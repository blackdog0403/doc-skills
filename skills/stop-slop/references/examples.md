# Examples

Before/after transformations demonstrating the Stop Slop rules.

## Binary Contrast → Direct Statement

**Before:**
> It's not about working harder. It's about working smarter.

**After:**
> Work smarter. Prioritize the tasks that compound.

---

## Throat-Clearing → Direct

**Before:**
> Here's the thing: most teams don't fail because of bad strategy. They fail because of bad execution.

**After:**
> Most teams fail at execution, not strategy.

---

## False Agency → Human Subject

**Before:**
> The data tells us that retention drops after day 7.

**After:**
> We measured a retention drop after day 7.

---

## Passive Voice → Active

**Before:**
> The decision was made to sunset the product.

**After:**
> The VP decided to sunset the product.

---

## Vague Declarative → Specific

**Before:**
> The implications are significant.

**After:**
> Losing this contract costs us $2M in annual revenue.

---

## Narrator-from-a-Distance → Reader in the Room

**Before:**
> Nobody designed this system to be fragile. It happened gradually.

**After:**
> You didn't design it to be fragile. Each shortcut felt small. Then one deploy broke everything.

---

## Staccato Fragmentation → Varied Rhythm

**Before:**
> Speed. That's it. That's the whole product.

**After:**
> The product sells speed, and customers pay because their current workflow takes three hours.

---

## Adverb-Heavy → Clean

**Before:**
> This is genuinely, fundamentally important to understand.

**After:**
> Understand this before you ship.
